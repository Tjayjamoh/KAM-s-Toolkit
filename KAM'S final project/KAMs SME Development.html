About KAM.

Established in 1959, Kenya Association of Manufacturers (KAM) is a representative of manufacturing and value-add industries in Kenya. The Association has grown into a dynamic, vibrant, credible Association that unites industrialists and offers a common voice for businesses.


We have been front and centre in driving fact-based policy advocacy towards the formation of industrial policies to strengthen and support the country’s economic development. Through fact-based advocacy, KAM partners with Government and its associated agencies to ensure a dynamic and flourishing manufacturing sector in Kenya, to realize a double-digit contribution to GDP.


Vision;To be a World Class BMO that effectively delivers services to its members.

Mission; To promote competitive and sustainable local manufacturing.

Goal;To transform KAM into a sustainable member-focused association delivering relevant, quality, timely and effective services to its members.


KAMs Sectors and Membership.

Kenya Association of Manufacturers (KAM) Members brings together over 1,400 manufacturing and service SMEs, organized into 14 sectors. The sectors are defined based on the UNIDO classification, and the type of raw materials respective companies import or the products they manufacture.


This way, members in a particular sector form a cohesive group with common issues of concern. This enables various sectors to prepare common policy positions for joint advocacy and negotiation with relevant government institutions in addressing issues of concern.


The Sectors are headed by Chairs, who are elected by members of a particular sector. The Sector Chairs are assisted by Vice-Chairs and Sector Officers. The Sectors are further broken down into Sub-sectors, which are categorized using the following criteria:

• Primary processor

• Process uniqueness

• Raw material commonality

• Product function

• Industry critical

KAM SME Development Hub.

Kenya Association of Manufacturers plays a crucial role in advocating the government to create an environment conducive to entrepreneurship and SME development to secure the future of industry. KAM set out to provide strategic leadership in supporting Manufacturing SMEs towards inclusive global competitiveness. SME development offers value added services to growth oriented and market ready manufacturing SMEs. It is geared towards preparing, nurturing and growing businesses to take full advantage of new emerging market frontiers both locally and in the international space.

The mission is to foster the development of a flourishing entrepreneurial culture and competitive small and medium manufacturers in Kenya and in the region.

KAM launched the Manufacturing SME Hub geared towards preparing, nurturing and growing manufacturing SMEs to take full advantage of the available markets.The Hub seeks to address the challenges affecting SMEs in the country including unfriendly policies and regulatory regime, tedious and lengthy process in quality standards and certifications, access to markets, access to affordable finance and poor governance structures.

The KAM SME HUB has been designed to offer a holistic approach to SME engagement. The Hub is structured to leverage on a variety of relevant partnerships to address specific needs through the Identification of priority and beneficial partnerships.

KAMs SMEs Innovation Programme.

The KAM SMEs Innovation programme is an intervention in partnership with GIZ, that seeks to support 50 Innovative SMEs through various targeted interventions that support the businesses overcome the unprecedented shocks. These interventions include but are not limited to:

•Conducting enterprise development (capacity building)

•Providing coaching /firm-level interventions

•Facilitating linkages to finance including liquidity and cash-flow support to MSMEs to ensure efficient operations and maintenance of income levels to sustain business and requisite employment.

•Undertaking performance assessment and impact reporting and recognition of their adaptation and innovativeness


The desired outcome for the entire programme is the SMEs engaged in this programme will have well-refined business plans, strategies and processes that are bankable and scalable.


KAMs Manufacturing Manifesto 2022-2027

In the wake of the disruptive Covid 19 pandemic, globalization and digitization the contribution of the Manufacturing sector has become a concern having not achieved the set national target of 15% of GDP by 2022. In efforts to accelerate the Growth, the role and Contribution of Manufacturing to Kenya’s GDP, the following issues are prioritized in KAMs.

Manufacturing Manifesto 2022-2027


Issue 1: Creating jobs in manufacturing

Issue 2: Raising export quantity and the intensity of Kenyan manufacturing

Issue 3: Reducing the regulatory burden on manufacturing

Issue 4: Raising investment in the manufacturing sector

Issue 5: Efficient provision of public goods and essential services

Issue 6: Effective and pro-industry national taxation structure

Issue 7: County industrial competitiveness

KAMs Manufacturing Priority Agenda 2022

In advancement of its advocacy and promoting manufacturing in Kenya, KAM has developed and published the Manufacturing Priority Agenda (MPA- 2022). Focusing on manufacturing sector recovery and sustained growth for Kenya’s shared prosperity, the MPA includes the following agenda under five (5) pillars in order to support manufacturing sector’s growth during uncertainties;


Pillar One: Competitiveness and level playing field

Agenda one: Improve regulatory efficiency

Agenda two: Promote access to quality, affordable and reliable energy for Manufacturing

Agenda three: Reduce transport and logistics costs

Agenda Four: Sustain the fight against illicit trade

Agenda five: Address multiple county charges, fees, and levies

Agenda seven: Incentivize prompt payment culture

Agenda eight: Avail long term financing to manufacturers


Pillar Two: Enhance market access

Agenda one: Enhance local market access

Agenda two: Promote regional market access

Agenda three: Diversify international market access


Pillar Three: Pro-industry policy and institutional framework

Agenda one: Ensure development of predictable and stable industrial policies through industry consultation

Agenda two: Ensure certainty and predictability of tax policies to encourage industrial investments

Agenda three: Ensure policy coherence between the two levels of government


Pillar Four: SMEs developmentk

Agenda one: Create a favorable policy environment for SMEs to flourish


Pillar Five: Industrial sustainability and resilience

Agenda one: Adoption of KAM's Industrial Manifesto proposals by political parties and both levels of government

Agenda two: Ensure peaceful 2022 general elections and transition

Agenda three: Ensure stable macroeconomic environment

Agenda four: Pro-industry skill development

Agenda five: Green growth and sustainable development

Agenda six: Accelerate SDG implementation

Agenda seven: Fight against corruption

Agenda eight: Fit-for-purpose public service

Agenda nine: Enhance Digitalization in Manufacturing Industry
